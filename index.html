MADISON WISCONSIN DREU

About

This Distributed Research Experience for Undergraduates (DREU) was funded by the Computing Research Association - Women (CRA-W). The DREU program allows students to travel to new universities and conduct research with volunteering professors. This summer I, Rebekah Manweiler, will be traveling to the University of Wisconsin - Madison to work with Professor Nicole Beckage in the field of computational modeling.

Rebekah Manweiler

Rebekah is an undergraduate student going into her fifth and final year at the University of Kansas. She is majoring in Computer Science and Minoring in Mathematics and Linguistics. Her research interests are in Psycholinguistics, Cognitive Science, Computational Modeling, Machine Learning, Language Acquisition, and the structure of the mental lexicon.

Professor Nicole Beckage

Nicole is a Research Professor at the University of Wisconsin - Madison in the Department of Psychology. She specializes in Computational Modeling, specifically cognitive models. Her current project focuses on modeling user interactions in the environment of GitHub, an open source software development platform.
